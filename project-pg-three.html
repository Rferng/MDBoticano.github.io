Matchmaker

Bringing Together Professors, Students, and Industry

Task User Research on...User Research

As the demand for user research expands, new methods must be explored in order to support the students who look to fill in the demand. But must the methods really be new? Before creating a new program to connect professors and students to real industry contexts, we are tasked with conducting research on how real our problem is, how others are trying to solve it, and how we might solve it ourselves.

Compare Current Solutions

True novelty is rare and maybe even impossible. Instead of doing research that's already been done, and creating a new program when there already exist some, we studied current attempts to connect industry, professors, and students together. We focused more on schools with design experience and curriculum already, as we wanted to find how their answers can be analyzed in the creation of ours.

Changing Perspectives

It's important to understand how other stakeholders view our project. The more that each stakeholder feels they are getting out of the project, the better we can move forward in creating a solution. Before we even began ideating on potential solutions, we sought out representatives of these stakeholders in order to understand what they each consider value. In summary of our findings, we then created a business model that we can use as a basis for the creating of our product and stakeholders' confidence in it. With a solid base and central point of discussion, future steps come with less risk.
