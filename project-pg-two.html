UCSD Votes!

Increasing Undergraduate Voter Turnout at UCSD

Task

Identify a contemporary issue. Conduct research and create a prototype for a possible solution to that issue. My group of five decided to tackle a local problem that permeates at a national scale: low voter turnout in the age group of 18-24 year olds. As college students ourselves, we would have our own peers to draw information from but we would not neglect how the issue exists in different age groups, states, and even countries.

Data Collection

Though we wanted to identify the problem at a large scale, we wanted to see how we could solve it at a local scale. So we looked to how the problem existed in the smaller local scale of just our university (UCSD). We conducted interviews with student organizations involved in political activities such as College Democrats, College Republics, specific candidate groups, and non-partisan groups aimed at increasing voter registration and turnout. We then grouped our data into three major problem areas: lack of information, inconvience, and apathy.

A Temporary Solution

One of our best tested solutions was the use of Snapchat filters. While there were already national political filters reminding people to register and to vote, we decided to extend the idea further by providing information of local resources to register and where they might go to vote based on the address they registed for. With the impending California Primary date, we were unable to evaluate the final results. However, as we were able to do a few iterations at least, we think that further refinement should lead to be a better outcome.
