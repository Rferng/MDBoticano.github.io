Understanding Designers' Search

An Ethnography

Task Ethanographic Interview

My partner and I interviewed professional designers for about half of a day to understand how they browsed the internet for answers to their industry problems. With the information we hope to collect, we want to discover how search might be improved for designers and in general.

Observe Real Behavior

We noticed that the usage of search paralleled the stage of production they were in. In their work cycle, they would start big on ideas and funnel into a solution. In how they searched, they would open numerous tabs which they would evaluate to get a broad sense of the topic they were researching, which they would then narrow down once they had a better idea.

Discover Hidden and Potential Insights

As industrial designers, they would often look for images models. They mentioned the struggle of finding the right thing, and how they often settled for what was the best that they had. They felt that an image based search would help them immensely. To their surprise, Google already had that feature. However, we never returned to determine if that realization had any effect on their productivity.
